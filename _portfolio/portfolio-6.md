## Overview
Genomic datasets are powerful but difficult to choose correctly due to scale, governance, inconsistent metadata, and population limitations. I developed a structured “five pillars” decision framework aimed at researchers who are strong in clinical or data-science domains but newer to genomics. :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}

## What the framework emphasises
- Starting with governance/ethics as a practical gatekeeper
- Translating “genomics complexity” into actionable checklist questions (data type, ancestry, sample size, readiness level, etc.)
- Making dataset discovery and feasibility assessment more systematic rather than ad-hoc :contentReference[oaicite:21]{index=21}
